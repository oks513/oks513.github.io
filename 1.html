Lindsey Stirling

Lindsey Stirling (born September 21, 1986) is an American violinist, songwriter, and dancer.[1][2][3] She presents choreographed violin performances, in live and music videos found on her official YouTube channel, which she created in 2007

Stirling
performs a variety of music styles, from classical to pop and rock to electronic dance music. Aside from original work, her discography contains covers of songs by other musicians and various soundtracks.[5][6] Her music video "Crystallize" finished as the eighth-most watched video of 2012 on YouTube,[7] and her cover version of "Radioactive" with Pentatonix won Response of the Year in the first YouTube Music Awards in 2013.[8] Stirling achieved one million singles sold worldwide by August 2014.[9] As of September 10, 2019, her Lindseystomp YouTube channel exceeded 12 million subscribers and over 3 billion total views.[10]

Stirling has been named in Forbes magazine's 30 Under 30 In Music: The Class Of 2015. Forbes notes her quarter-finalist position on America's Got Talent season five in 2010,[11][12] a No. 2 position on the Billboard 200 for her second album Shatter Me in 2014, and her 11 million subscribers on YouTube.[13]

Stirling's eponymous debut album was a commercial success in Europe, selling 200,000 copies in Germany, winning a platinum certification; three additional certifications were given by Austria, Switzerland and Poland. The album was nominated for the 2014 Billboard Music Awards for Top Dance/Electronic Albums.[14] Stirling's second album Shatter Me won Top Dance/Electronic Album at the 2015 Billboard Music Awards.[15]

Early life

Stirling is the middle child of three daughters of Stephen and Diane Stirling. Stirling describes her childhood as being raised in a modest household and stated "I would not trade my humble childhood years for anything else."[16] Due to her family's financial limitations, her parents could only afford to find a violin teacher who would give her half lessons. Although they were told by instructors that "a child isn't going to learn how to play in 15 minutes a week", her parents persisted, and at the age of five she began taking violin lessons.[17]

Living in Gilbert, Arizona, Stirling attended Greenfield Junior High, and at age sixteen,[18] Stirling attended Mesquite High School and joined four friends in a rock band called Stomp on Melvin. As part of her experience with the group, Stirling wrote a solo violin rock song, and her performance helped her to win the state title of Arizona's Junior Miss and claim the Spirit Award in the America's Junior Miss Finals competition.[19] Stirling was also a member of Charley Jenkins Band for about a year.[20]

From a young age, Stirling had a fascination for dance, and desired to take both dancing and violin lessons. In an interview with NewMediaRockstars, she said, "...ever since I was a kid, I've always wished that I could dance, but my parents said, 'You [can] choose violin or you [can] choose dance, but we can't afford both', and I chose violin. So this is kind of a fulfillment – it's funny to say, but this is something I've always wanted to do."[21] Stirling registered her YouTube channel on May 20, 2007, under the username lindseystomp

Personal life

Stirling attended Brigham Young University (BYU) in Provo, Utah, to study film making.[16] She later served as a missionary in New York City for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church).[136][137] A story she wrote about her mission was later published in the compilation Do Not Attemp in Heels: Mission Stories and Advice from Sisters Who've Been There.[138] Stirling returned to Provo in 2009 to continue studying at BYU. She then moved back to Arizona, in December 2012, to be with her family. In August 2015, she graduated from the BYU Marriott School of Management with a Bachelor of Science degree in recreation management.[139] She resides in Los Angeles.[140]

For a short time she dated film maker Devin Graham, who had attended the same university and church.[141] The two began dating shortly after the filming of "Crystallize" and Graham moved to Utah.[142][143] The two have since ended their professional and personal relationship.[143] In October 2017, on "Dancing with the Stars" Stirling revealed her year and a half relationship with Ryan Weed ended.[144]

Stirling has publicly spoken about her battle with anorexia. She discovered her disorder while working for a treatment center for troubled girls. Stirling said in an interview with Good Morning America that her song "Shatter Me" was "actually my story of overcoming my eating disorder".[145] The cover art of the album is a reference to her struggle which showcases a "seemingly perfect-looking ballerina standing in the middle of a cracked glass globe".[146] In 2013, Stirling was featured by the LDS Church in its I'm a Mormon campaign in which she spoke openly about how her faith helped in her battle during high school and college.[16][142] On November 25, 2014, she performed and answered questions in a live world broadcast to LDS youth.[147] A one-hour video recording of the event was featured on the LDS Church home page.[148]

Stirling has a celestial body named after her, Asteroid 242516 Lindseystirling, about 2–3 km diameter, semi-major axis of 2.72AU, eccentricity of 0.051, inclined 3.7 degrees to ecliptic.[149]
